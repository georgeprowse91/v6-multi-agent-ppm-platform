## 1) Strategy Overview

### 1.1 Executive Summary
PwC has an opportunity to lead the AI-native enterprise PPM market with a multi-agent platform that unifies fragmented project and portfolio data across systems.

The platform is positioned as an **orchestration layer** that works with existing enterprise tools (e.g., Planview, Jira, SAP, Workday), rather than replacing them.

### 1.2 Strategic Opportunity
- **PPM software market (2024):** $14.3B, growing at 13.5% CAGR.
- **AI-powered PPM segment:** $2.1B, fastest-growing subsegment.
- **Competitive white space:** No established enterprise-grade multi-agent PPM leader.
- **PwC advantage:** Domain credibility, implementation capability, and existing enterprise relationships.

### 1.3 Recommended Commercial Approach
- **Primary model:** Managed Service (SaaS) with tiered agent packaging.
- **Secondary model:** Private cloud deployment + licensing + services.
- **Strategic model (later phase):** Partnership/JV channel with major PPM vendors.

### 1.4 Year 3 Revenue Outlook (Target)
- 50 enterprise clients at $400K–$1.2M annual recurring revenue each: **$30M–$60M ARR**.
- Implementation services: **$25M–$40M**.
- **Total potential by Year 3:** **$55M–$100M**.

## 2) Market Problem and Why It Matters

### 2.1 The Integration Complexity Crisis
Large enterprises commonly operate across 10–25 disconnected systems for:
- Portfolio planning
- Project execution
- ERP/finance
- Resources/HRIS
- Collaboration and documentation
- Risk, compliance, and vendor operations

### 2.2 Observable Business Impact
- PMO analysts spend up to **40%** of time on manual aggregation.
- Executive dashboards are often **1–2 weeks stale**.
- Decisions are made with incomplete cross-system visibility.
- Governance and audit exposure increases due to process inconsistency.

### 2.3 Why Existing Options Underperform
1. **Traditional PPM suites**
   - High cost and long implementation cycles (18–36 months).
   - Often require standardising diverse project types into rigid models.
2. **“AI add-on” platforms**
   - AI is usually limited to chatbots/basic analytics.
   - Limited orchestration across enterprise system landscapes.
3. **Custom integration middleware**
   - Improves data movement, but not decision intelligence.
   - High build/maintenance cost and low reusability.

### 2.4 Unmet Need
Enterprises need a platform that:
- Orchestrates existing systems.
- Synthesises data into real-time portfolio insights.
- Embeds governance in day-to-day workflow.
- Recommends next-best actions with transparent AI.
- Adapts to industry, methodology, and enterprise stack.

## 4) Competitive Landscape

### 4.1 Market Tiers
- **Tier 1: Traditional Enterprise PPM (~$8B).**
- **Tier 2: Collaborative Work Management (~$4B).**
- **Tier 3: AI-Enhanced PPM (~$2.1B, ~35% CAGR).**

### 4.2 Relative Position
- Traditional vendors are strong in workflow coverage but weaker in AI-native orchestration.
- Emerging AI features are often assistive, not operationally embedded.
- Custom consulting solutions are expensive and non-repeatable.
- This platform’s intended position: **high integration breadth + high AI intelligence**.

### 4.3 Window of Opportunity
Expected competitive convergence in 12–18 months. Priority is to establish category leadership early through client references, repeatable delivery, and partner leverage.

## 6) Commercialisation Models

### 6.1 Model A (Primary): Managed Service (SaaS)
**Commercial mechanics**
- Subscription pricing by project volume, users, and enabled agent bundles.
- Add-ons for premium industry agents and premium support.

**Economics**
- Implementation: $500K–$1.5M (one-time).
- Recurring: $30K–$100K MRR/client.
- Gross margin target: 70–80%.

**Pros**
- Predictable recurring revenue.
- Faster adoption and upgrades.
- Strong land-and-expand profile.

**Risks**
- Data residency constraints for specific sectors.
- Certification and compliance overhead.

### 6.2 Model B (Secondary): Private Cloud + License
- Annual license + implementation + ongoing support.
- Better fit for strict security/residency environments.
- Higher upfront revenue, lower recurring yield.

### 6.3 Model C (Strategic): Partner/JV Channel
- Co-sell/co-deliver with major PPM ecosystem players.
- Trade-off: accelerated access vs. revenue share and roadmap influence.

### 6.4 Model D (Future): Agent Marketplace
- Ecosystem-led expansion of industry/function agents.
- Requires governance, QA controls, and developer ecosystem maturity.

### 6.5 Recommended Sequencing
1. **Year 1:** SaaS-managed service with design partners.
2. **Year 2:** Add private cloud option for regulated buyers.
3. **Year 3:** Activate strategic partner channels.
4. **Year 4+:** Evaluate/open agent marketplace.
