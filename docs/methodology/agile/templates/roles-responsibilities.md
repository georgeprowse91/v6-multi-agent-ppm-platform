# Roles & Responsibilities Matrix (Agile)

These role definitions provide a baseline for Agile delivery. Teams should tailor titles,
responsibilities, and decision rights to match their operating model and product domain.

| Role | Description | Key Responsibilities | Decision Authority | Escalation Path |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product Owner | Voice of the customer who owns value delivery and backlog priorities. | Defines product vision, manages and prioritizes backlog, clarifies acceptance criteria, accepts completed work. | Decide backlog priority and release scope within agreed constraints. | Escalate major scope/value trade-offs to Sponsor or governance body. |
| Scrum Master | Servant leader who enables team effectiveness and adherence to Agile values. | Facilitates ceremonies, removes impediments, coaches team and stakeholders on Agile practices, protects focus. | Decide process adjustments within Agile framework. | Escalate systemic impediments to Delivery Lead or Sponsor. |
| Agile Team Member (Developers, Testers, Designers) | Cross-functional contributors delivering increments of value. | Co-create requirements, design and build solutions, test and validate, refine estimates, deliver increments. | Decide technical implementation approach and task execution. | Escalate technical blockers or dependencies to Scrum Master/Product Owner. |
| Stakeholder | Business or technical partner affected by or contributing to the product. | Provide feedback, clarify needs, participate in reviews, validate outcomes. | Influence priority through feedback and business impact. | Escalate unmet needs to Product Owner. |
| Sponsor | Executive accountable for outcomes and investment. | Secures funding, sets strategic objectives, resolves organizational conflicts. | Approve major budget or strategic shifts. | Escalate to executive leadership or steering committee. |

## Usage Notes
- Keep the backlog transparent and continuously refined.
- The team collectively defines requirements and acceptance criteria during refinement.
